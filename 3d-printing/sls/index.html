SLS-Printable Zometool Extensions

For all of the remarkable expressiveness of The Zometool, there are many ways it can be extended. Over the years, I have used vZome to design many such extensions. I'm offering all of those designs here, free for your use, to print yourself if you own a selective laser sintering (SLS) 3D printer, or to upload to any printing service like Shapeways. (The prior instantiation of Shapeways had a marketplace, and I used to make all of these models available for sale there, with no markup.)

Uses

The most common kind of extension here is custom struts. Some of them are in the standard directions, but in custom lengths not offered by Zometool. Others attach to the ball in one of the three standard holes, but from there they "jog", taking a different direction relative to the icosahedral symmetry of the Zome ball.

A second kind of extension here is ball replacements. Often when building Zometool models of 4D polytope projections to 3D, projected struts will intersect each other, and you really don't want to use a Zome ball there, since it is not a vertex of the 4D polytope. Due to a Zometool tradition, these ball replacements are called "bobs".

Finally, there are a handful of oddities for very specific situations, for example modeling a shape that requires two green struts and a red strut to all share the same hole in a Zome ball.

See the description of each design to learn the context where it is useful.

Caveats and Provisos

STL is a fairly dumb format, and it cannot carry any color information. The designs here are all shown in vZome-relevant colors, but do not expect your printed parts to magically come out in those colors! If you print in white nylon material, you can generally use fabric dye (like Rit) to achieve the color you want. Some 3D printing services also offer to dye your models in certain colors, but be careful -- all these designs will look alike as white nylon to the printing technicians, so if you order multiple colored prints at the same time they will often get the colors wrong.

These designs were specifically designed to lower the cost of printing with Shapeways, which is why the designs are generally grouped and joined together. Other printing services may have different cost criteria.

None of these designs are really useful for printing on a typical home 3D printer that uses fused deposition modeling (FDM). I do have a few designs for that purpose, and I'll try to get a page for those put up soon.

I'm happy to offer advice on printing, dyeing, or designing your own objects using vZome, and I always like hearing from vZome users. Find me as "Scott V" on the vZome Discord server.
